Reviews

Amazónico

Amazónico in DIFC immerses you in lush greenery, evoking jungle vibes without the humidity or wildlife. This expansive, three-floor venue offers diverse dining spots, from the rooftop to the sushi bar. Despite its popularity and constant buzz, the efficient service ensures a seamless experience. Designed by Lázaro Rosa-Violán, the space features stunning 3D butterfly art installations. The rooftop bar, now Paraíso Rooftop Club, and an expanded sushi counter highlight its appeal.

Em Sherif Cafe

Downtown Dubai is brimming with Lebanese restaurants, but Em Sherif Cafe stands out. Known for its opulence in Beirut, the Dubai branch offers a more relaxed, home-style dining experience. Enjoy a glass of Lebanese grape with dishes designed for sharing. Start with creamy hummus and crispy falafel, then savor the bokjat jibineh cheese pie, seasoned with mint, parsley, and olive. The mixed grill plate, featuring tender tawook, kebab, and beef skewers, is a must-try. Portions are generous, so expect leftovers. Em Sherif Cafe exemplifies how Lebanese food should be: meticulously prepared and delightful to eat.

Prime68

Prime68, located on the 68th floor of the JW Marriott Marquis, breaks the mold of traditional steakhouses with its modern design and stunning floor-to-ceiling windows offering breathtaking views of Jumeirah and the Arabian Gulf. Despite its contemporary look, it retains the essence of a classic steakhouse, featuring a corridor lined with fine bottles and dry-aged meats. The menu is extensive, ranging from USDA Prime Black Angus filet mignons to dry-aged Sanchoku wagyu tomahawks. Prime68 caters to both casual steak lovers and connoisseurs of the finest meats. Start your meal with the Maryland crab cake, accompanied by a fennel-radish slaw and pickled mustard, or dive straight into the tangy steak tartare for a robust flavor experience.
